Welcome to the Statue of Unity

The Statue of Unity is a tribute to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, a leader who united India. Explore the world’s tallest statue and learn about the man behind the vision.The project was first announced in 2010, and construction started in October 2013 by Indian company Larsen & Toubro, with a total construction cost of ₹27 billion (US$422 million). It was designed by Indian sculptor Ram V. Sutar and was inaugurated by the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi, on 31 October 2018, the 143rd anniversary of Patel's birth.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel: A Leader of Unmatched Vision

Early Life

Born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, Gujarat, Sardar Patel was a bright student who later became a lawyer and a leader with a strong sense of duty towards his nation.

Freedom Struggle

Patel played a crucial role in India’s struggle for independence, working alongside Mahatma Gandhi. His leadership in the Kheda and Bardoli Satyagraha earned him the title ‘Sardar’.

Unifier of India

As India’s first Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Home Affairs, Patel was instrumental in the integration of over 560 princely states into the Indian Union.

A Lasting Legacy

Sardar Patel’s vision of a united India remains his greatest legacy, celebrated today through the Statue of Unity, standing as a symbol of national integrity.

In His Own Words

‘Manpower without unity is not a strength unless it is harmonized and united properly, then it becomes a spiritual power.’
